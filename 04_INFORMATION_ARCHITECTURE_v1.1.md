## 2. Navigation Structure

Our navigation philosophy replaces the complex directories of massive online stores with an unhurried, clean orientation.

### 2.1 Luxury Announcement Bar
* **Purpose:** Communicate essential brand values, shipping terms, or prestigious updates without looking commercial.
* **Behavior:** A very thin, high-contrast bar at the absolute top of the viewport. Rather than flashing or sliding rapidly, it cycles between statements via a slow, soft 1.2s dissolve fade every 8 seconds. It can be collapsed by clicking a minimal custom cross icon on the far right.
* **Content Strategy:** Avoid "Sale" or "Discount" copy. Instead, focus on shipping, gifting options, or collection drops.
* **Examples:** 
  * *"Complimentary fully insured delivery across India."*
  * *"Experience the Temple Gold Collection — Curated for the modern bride."*
  * *"Complimentary handwritten gift wrapping on all bespoke selections."*

### 2.2 Header Navigation (Desktop)
* **Left Aligned:** Primary collections pathways: `Shop` (Dropdown), `Collections` (Lookbooks), `Bridal Salon` (Specialty), `Our Story` (Heritage).
* **Center Aligned:** The Avighna Collections logo—prominent and serving as a home link.
* **Right Aligned:** Customer utilities: `Search` (Icon), `Concierge` (Text link), `Wishlist` (Heart icon), `Cart` (Bag icon with item count).

### 2.3 Mega Menu Structure
When hovering over `Shop`, a clean, full-width panel slides down slowly. It is divided into three distinct zones:
* **Zone 1 (Left - 50%): Categories Grid.** Clean text links with generous vertical line height (Necklaces, Earrings, Bangles, Rings).
* **Zone 2 (Middle - 25%): Featured Collection Link.** A single, stylized title card (e.g., "The Temple Gold Edit") with a brief descriptive sentence.
* **Zone 3 (Right - 25%): The Heritage Image.** A high-resolution editorial portrait of a woman wearing a signature collection piece, establishing immediate visual context.

### 2.4 Mobile Navigation
* **Top Bar:** Menu button (Hamburger) on the left, logo in the center, and Cart icon on the right.
* **Slide-out Drawer:** A full-height panel sliding from the left. Navigation items are arranged vertically with large line height. The bottom of the drawer includes links for `WhatsApp Concierge` and `Store Location`.
* **Sticky Bottom Utility Bar:** A thin bar containing `Search`, `Wishlist`, `Shop`, and `Account` for easy thumb access.

### 2.5 Sticky Navigation Behavior
* As the user scrolls down, the header slowly dissolves.
* When the user scrolls *up* even slightly, the header slides back down gently, switching to a glass-morphic background (semi-transparent with blur) to maintain visibility without obscuring page content.

### 2.6 Search Behavior
* Clicking the Search icon opens a full-screen, clean overlay.
* The screen fades to a soft bone-white tint. The search input appears at the top center in large typography.
* Before the user types, it displays "Suggested Discoveries" (e.g., *Temple Chokers*, *Polki Earrings*, *Bridal Sets*).
* Results appear instantly as the user types, presented in an editorial column layout (image, name, collection) rather than a raw list.

### 2.7 Premium Footer Architecture
The footer has been restructured to emphasize trust, heritage, and relationship building, replacing commercial maps with high-prestige editorial blocks:
* **Zone A: Brand Promise & Statement (Full Width Top).** A stylized block presenting the brand's core mission: *"Crafted with reverence. Designed to make you feel like royalty every single day."*
* **Zone B: Customer Care & Concierge (Column 1).** Care Guide, Lifetime Polish Guarantee, returns instructions, and a quick WhatsApp Consultation trigger.
* **Zone C: The Collections (Column 2).** Links to lookbooks (Temple Gold, Antique, Minimalist, Bridal Salon).
* **Zone D: Store & Appointment (Column 3).** Store locator, booking links, hours of operation.
* **Zone E: The Newsletter Invitation (Column 4).** A clean, formless-looking input field requesting "An invitation to your inbox" to receive editorial collection launches.
* **Zone F: Social Media (Column 5).** Clean text links (Pinterest, Instagram, YouTube) in place of unstyled colorful icons.
* **Bottom Bar:** Copyright and payment signatures presented in a solid, low-contrast dark tint.

### Section 01: The Hero
* **Purpose:** Establish brand status and arrest attention immediately.
* **User Goal:** Understand the brand's identity and navigate directly into the shop or collections.
* **Business Goal:** Minimize bounce rate and immediately filter traffic into high-value categories.
* **Why it appears here:** It is the threshold. It must set the aesthetic benchmark and color tone before any other information is introduced.

### Section 02: The Brand Belief
* **Purpose:** Introduce the brand philosophy ("Democratizing luxury for every Indian woman").
* **User Goal:** Align emotionally with the brand's values.
* **Business Goal:** Separate the brand from cheap costume jewellery competitors.
* **Why it appears here:** Immediately follows the visual welcome to provide intellectual context to the beauty just witnessed.

### Section 03: The Editorial Brand Film
* **Purpose:** Immerse the customer in the sensory world of Avighna—metal-working, hand-polishing, draping, and soft lighting.
* **Placement:** Positioned immediately following the brand belief statement to reinforce it with cinematic weight.
* **Transition:** Fades in softly as the scroll approaches, playing silently and loopable in a widescreen cinematic ratio. It features a subtle button on the bottom corner to "Open Film" (expand to full screen with atmospheric sitar sound).
* **Why video is important:** Luxury is visual and atmospheric. A static image can suggest a product, but a moving, cinematic film builds a world the customer wants to be part of.

### Section 04: The Collections Map
* **Purpose:** Direct users to our curated collections (Temple Gold, Antique, Minimalist).
* **User Goal:** Find a specific style or aesthetic path.
* **Business Goal:** Filter users by design preference early in the journey.
* **Why it appears here:** Acts as the physical guidepost in the boutique foyer, showing the rooms available to explore.

### Section 05: The Bridal Experience
* **Purpose:** Position Avighna as a premium choice for Indian bridal jewelry.
* **Storytelling Approach:** Framing the sets not as simple items, but as part of a bride's heritage. Focus on the emotional weight of "The Trousseau" and "The Sangeet."
* **CTA Strategy:** Avoid "Shop Now." Instead, use "Explore the Trousseau Edit" or "Request a Private Bridal Consultation," directing to a dedicated form/WhatsApp experience.
* **Why it appears here:** Positioned early in the scroll to immediately establish the high-prestige, heritage aspect of the catalog.

### Section 06: The Featured Editorial
* **Purpose:** Showcase a single signature set in an editorial layout.
* **User Goal:** Imagine the jewellery worn in real life.
* **Business Goal:** Drive clicks to our highest-prestige product pages.
* **Why it appears here:** Creates visual variation after the structure of the Collections Map, restoring emotional intrigue.

### Section 07: The Craft Story
* **Purpose:** Demonstrate the artisan craftsmanship behind the 1 gram gold composition.
* **User Goal:** Confirm the physical quality and origin of the items.
* **Business Goal:** Build trust in our materials to support high conversion rates.
* **Why it appears here:** Appears mid-page to address quality questions that arise once visual desire is established.

### Section 08: The Curated Selection
* **Purpose:** Present a clean carousel of individual hero products.
* **User Goal:** Quickly scan pricing, design range, and add-to-wishlist options.
* **Business Goal:** Highlight individual best-sellers.
* **Why it appears here:** Transition from brand storytelling to product interaction, preparing the user to shop.

### Section 09: The Occasion Context
* **Purpose:** Organize products by life events (Wedding, Festival, Daily Wear, Office, Gift).
* **User Goal:** Find pieces matching specific wardrobe needs.
* **Business Goal:** Increase average order value by suggesting complete sets for specific events.
* **Why it appears here:** Capitalizes on shopping intent by framing items within the customer's personal calendar.

### Section 10: The Boutique & Appointment Visit
* **Purpose:** Bridge the digital experience with the brand's physical boutique presence in Bangalore.
* **Layout:** A clean split-panel section. On the left: Google Maps interface in custom slate tones, opening hours, address details. On the right: A direct, elegant invitation card to "Book a Private Styling Session."
* **Why it appears here:** Positions Avighna as a real, premium boutique entity with an physical space, building trust before customer reviews.

### Section 11: The Voices
* **Purpose:** Show genuine customer letters and styling reviews.
* **User Goal:** Read real experiences from other brides and gift-givers.
* **Business Goal:** Overcome hesitation through social proof.
* **Why it appears here:** Placed near the footer to reassure the user as they approach the end of the homepage scroll.

### Section 12: The Promise
* **Purpose:** Highlight logistics and guarantees (Lifetime polish guarantee, free secure shipping).
* **User Goal:** Confirm shipping times and return policies.
* **Business Goal:** Remove operational friction.
* **Why it appears here:** Placed at the bottom to remove residual hesitation before the final call to action.

### Section 13: The Journal
* **Purpose:** Share styling tips, heritage essays, and wedding culture.
* **User Goal:** Learn about jewelry styling and heritage.
* **Business Goal:** Build SEO authority and return traffic.
* **Why it appears here:** Offers a non-commercial exit point for users who are not yet ready to buy.

### Section 14: The Invitation
* **Purpose:** A clean newsletter signup form styled like a personal letter.
* **User Goal:** Join the inner circle of the brand.
* **Business Goal:** Build an email list for long-term customer relationships.
* **Why it appears here:** The final touchpoint of the page, acting as an invitation to stay connected.

## 4. Shop Page Structure

The Shop page balances high-end curation with functional search capability.

* **Top Bar:** Category filter tabs (All, Necklaces, Earrings, Bangles, Rings) with a simple count next to each category.
* **Sidebar (Slide-out filter on mobile, clean left column on desktop):**
  * *Filter by Metal Polish:* Temple Yellow Gold, Rose Gold, Antique Gold.
  * *Filter by Stone:* Uncut Polki, Kemp Stones, Pearl, None.
  * *Filter by Price:* Clean ranges (Under ₹2,000, ₹2,000 - ₹5,000, ₹5,000+).
* **Grid Layout:** Asymmetric layout. Instead of a standard 4x4 grid of identical squares, we feature larger cards for signature pieces and smaller cards for accessories, mixed with visual editorial blocks.
* **Product Card Content:** Image (fades to secondary on hover), name, collection tag, price, and a subtle add-to-wishlist heart.

## 5. Collection Page Structure

Designed as a digital lookbook.

* **Hero Section:** Full-width editorial image or slow-motion video showing the collection theme. Overlay features the collection name and a short narrative paragraph.
* **The Narrative Anchor:** A block of text that tells the historical or cultural story of the collection design.
* **Asymmetrical Product Grid:** Curated sets are showcased together. For example, a necklace and matching jhumkas are displayed in side-by-side cards, framed by whitespace.
* **Styling Guide Block:** A visual block showing how to layer pieces from this collection together.

## 7. Operational & Luxury Experience Flows

### 7.1 Cart Flow
* **Trigger:** Customer clicks "Add to Velvet Tray" on a product card or detail page.
* **Action:** A clean slide-out cart drawer emerges from the right. The background of the page dims slightly.
* **Drawer Content:**
  * Selected items presented with high-quality thumbnails, title, price, and a simple quantity adjuster.
  * A prompt offering complimentary handwritten gift notes.
  * Clear display of complimentary shipping.
  * A single, clean CTA button: "Proceed to Checkout."

### 7.2 Checkout Flow
A secure, quiet, and distraction-free checkout experience:
* **Header:** Minimal header showing only the brand monogram and a link to return to the cart.
* **Step 1: Contact & Delivery:** Email/phone input and shipping address fields. Use clean, single-column forms with floating labels to prevent visual noise.
* **Step 2: Shipping Method:** Defaulted to "Complimentary Insured Delivery."
* **Step 3: Secure Payment:** Seamless integration of Razorpay. No redirection; the payment modal opens directly within our styled environment.
* **Confirmation (Thank You Page):** A clean thank-you letter from the founders, detailing order tracking steps and showcasing the unboxing expectations.

### 7.3 Customer Account Flow
* **Entry:** Clicking the Account utility icon in the header.
* **The Dashboard:** Clean dashboard showing:
  * *The Curated Chest (Wishlist):* Saved items with sharing links.
  * *Order History:* Direct tracking links and invoice downloads.
  * *Concierge Support:* One-click link to start a WhatsApp discussion regarding their order.

### 7.4 Mobile Navigation Flow
* **Trigger:** Click the top-left menu icon.
* **Interaction:** Nav slides out, occupying 85% of screen width.
* **Hierarchy:**
  1. Main navigation categories (Shop, Collections, Story).
  2. Sub-categories revealed via clean, accordion-style expansion.
  3. Bottom utilities (WhatsApp Concierge, Store Locator, Client FAQ).

## 8. Specialty Luxury Experiences

### 8.1 Luxury Concierge
* **The Concept:** A client should never feel isolated in front of a shopping cart. The digital flagship incorporates a real-time concierge desk.
* **WhatsApp Desk:** Direct, unhurried routing to a personal stylist who can share real-time pictures of jewelry under different light, coordinate custom bridal measurements, or help select gifts.
* **Phone Consultations:** Request a scheduled callback for dedicated wedding trousseau consultations.

### 8.2 Luxury Gift Experience
* **The Packaging:** Every item is shipped in a hard-shell box, wrapped in silk tissue, scented with sandalwood, and secured with a custom wax seal.
* **Bespoke Gift Messages:** During checkout/cart, users can opt for a calligraphy card with a custom message, written by hand by our team.
* **Wedding Gifting Suite:** Curated gift boxes containing coordinated sets for bridesmaids, mothers of the bride, and key family members, complete with special presentation options.
* **Festival Gifting Portal:** Pre-packaged traditional pairings for Diwali, Rakhi, and Navratri, eliminating styling guesswork.

### 8.3 Appointment Experience
* **The Concept:** Connect the digital space to physical styling via booking.
* **Service Paths:**
  * *Book Boutique Visit:* Schedule a time to visit the physical Bangalore store, ensuring a designated stylist is reserved with a styling desk ready.
  * *Bespoke Virtual Styling:* Book a video call consultation to view pieces on camera.
  * *Wedding Consultation:* A 45-minute dedicated discussion for the bride and her family to match jewelry with wedding ensembles.
* **Flow:** Clean date/time picker ➔ Name & Phone ➔ Quick questionnaire regarding outfit colors ➔ Automated styling confirmation.

### 8.4 Premium FAQ Experience
* **Layout Design:** A clean, spacious page featuring a custom luxury accordion. Clicking a question reveals the answer via a smooth ease-out vertical expansion.
* **Content Grouping:**
  * *Material & Composition:* Composition of 1 gram gold, core alloy, tarnishing prevention, and skincare safety.
  * *Shipping & Security:* Transit insurance details, delivery speeds, and tracking methods.
  * *Care & Guarantee:* Repair services, re-polishing procedures, and warranty terms.

### 8.5 Occasion-Based Shopping
We organize categories not just by product type, but by the customer's social calendar:
* **Wedding:** High-prestige, layered temple and antique gold sets designed to photograph beautifully.
* **Festival:** Culturally authentic necklaces and statement pieces matching traditional silks.
* **Daily Wear:** Durable, lightweight accessories designed for versatility.
* **Office:** Minimalist, clean earrings, rings, and chains that convey quiet sophistication.
* **Gift:** Coordinated sets and gift sets designed to fit standard sizes, minimizing returns.

## 9. Navigation Tactics

### 9.1 Breadcrumb Strategy
* **Visual Style:** Small, low-contrast, light sans-serif text placed above the main title.
* **Format:** `Home / Shop / Necklaces / Temple Gold Choker`
* **Behavior:** Clicking a parent link returns the user with the active filter state preserved, avoiding page resets.

### 9.2 Search Journey
* **Phase 1: Activation.** Click search icon ➔ full-screen overlay opens ➔ background dims.
* **Phase 2: Suggestion.** System suggests categories and recent hot searches.
* **Phase 3: Typings.** Results update instantly.
* **Phase 4: Navigation.** Click result to go directly to product page, or click "View All Results" to go to a styled Search Results page.

### 9.3 Wishlist Journey
* **Phase 1: Saving.** Clicking the heart icon on any card adds the item to the wishlist. The icon shifts color with a subtle fade animation.
* **Phase 2: Review.** Customer accesses "My Curated Chest" via the header icon.
* **Phase 3: Action.** Items can be added to the cart, shared with friends/partners, or saved for upcoming occasions.

## 11. Creative Director Review

### Self-Challenge & Critique
* **The "Accessible Luxury" Paradox:** The brand aims to sell 1 gram gold jewellery (accessible) while maintaining the prestige of Cartier. If the visual design is too austere, it may intimidate the aspirational buyer who seeks value. If it is too commerce-focused, it loses its premium charm.
* **The Solution:** We must frame the value not as a "discount" but as "democratized art." The website should look like a luxury gallery, but the copy should invite the user in, making her feel welcome rather than excluded. We must use storytelling to show that the low price is a deliberate brand mission, not a reflection of low quality.
* **The Grid Dilemma:** Standard e-commerce layouts rely on boring grids of square boxes. While highly functional, this destroys the boutique feel.
* **The Solution:** Introduce asymmetrical layout elements. Place a large editorial image next to a product detail, or use alternating card sizes. This mimics the physical boutique, where some items are highlighted in glass cases while others are arranged in drawers.
